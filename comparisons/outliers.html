Comparisons - Outliers

Chart 1 - Identifying Outliers by Year, 2000-2014
It is clear this dataset has some extreme outliers in duration of sightings, especially with respect to years 2002 (shape = light); 2010 (shape = no shape provided); and 2012 (shape = light). Ideally you'd want to exclude these outliers prior to further analysis so long as there is no specific reason to keep the observations in the dataset.

Chart 2 - Identifying Outliers by Weekday
Here, Monday = 1. This chart shows the major outliers identified previously (occurring in 2002, 2010, 2012) seem to have occurred on a Thursday, Friday, Saturday, and Sunday. Until these outliers are dropped, there is no reason to proceed with analysis.

Chart 3 - Finding & Dropping the Major Outliers
After examining the dataset in Excel, you can see the top 3 outliers are in the years where disturbances were identified in Chart 1 (occurring in 2002, 2010, 2012), and all observations in the top 10 fall on the days identified in Chart 2 (Thurs-Sun).

Furthermore, after examining the notes field included in the dataset, in most cases of sightings reported of durations longer than 5 hours were cases where the observer reported seeing objects on multiple nights. I decided to drop all observations from the dataset that were for durations over 5 hours long. This reduced the size of the dataset from 56,739 to 56,451 (-288 obsv).
